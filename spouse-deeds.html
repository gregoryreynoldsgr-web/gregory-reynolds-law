Adding a Spouse to a Maryland Deed

Many Maryland homeowners purchase a home before marriage and later decide that they want their husband or wife added to the deed. Others want both spouses reflected on title for estate planning, family planning, or peace of mind. In many cases, this can be done with a properly prepared and recorded Maryland deed.

A spouse deed is not simply a “form.” The deed needs to correctly identify the current owner, the new ownership structure, the legal description, the county land records reference, and the proper tax exemption language when available. The deed also needs to be signed, notarized, submitted with the proper intake sheet, and recorded in the appropriate Maryland county land records.

Why homeowners add a spouse to title

  • To make sure both spouses are legally reflected as owners of the home.
  • To simplify ownership after marriage when one spouse bought the home before the wedding.
  • To help avoid confusion if one spouse passes away.
  • To coordinate the deed with broader estate planning goals.
  • To make sure the land records match the family’s current intent.
  • To clean up title before refinancing, selling, or preparing additional estate planning documents.

Maryland background: tenancy by the entirety

In Maryland, married couples often choose to hold property as tenants by the entirety. This is a form of ownership available only to married spouses. It generally means both spouses own the property together as one marital unit, and when one spouse passes away, the surviving spouse continues owning the property without the need for the deceased spouse’s interest to pass through probate.

That is one of the main reasons spouse deeds are so popular. When done correctly, adding a spouse can create a cleaner ownership structure, reduce uncertainty, and help avoid unnecessary problems later. It is especially important when the property is the family home.

Possible benefits of adding your spouse

Every situation is different, but the common benefits are practical. A properly prepared spouse deed can place both spouses on record title, clarify ownership, support estate planning, and potentially allow the property to pass more smoothly to the surviving spouse. For many families, the deed is part of getting the house, marriage, and estate plan all pointed in the same direction.

For example, if one spouse purchased the home years before marriage, the land records may still show only that spouse as the owner. That may be perfectly legal, but it may no longer match what the couple wants. A spouse deed can update the ownership so the deed reflects the marriage and the couple’s current plans.

What we usually need to prepare the deed

  • The property address.
  • The current deed or land records reference, if available.
  • The full legal names of both spouses.
  • How the spouses want to hold title.
  • Whether there is an existing mortgage.
  • Whether the deed is being done for estate planning, refinancing, divorce, or another reason.

Removing a spouse or former spouse from a Maryland deed

We also prepare deeds removing a spouse or former spouse from title. This often comes up after divorce, separation, refinancing, or a written agreement between the parties. In many cases, one spouse signs a deed transferring their interest to the other spouse.

Removing a spouse from a deed should be handled carefully. The deed changes ownership of the property, but it does not automatically remove a person from the mortgage loan. If there is an existing mortgage, the deed and loan issues should be considered together so there are no surprises later.
