Why attorney-prepared deeds matter

Deeds may look simple, but mistakes can create real problems. A defective deed, incorrect name, wrong ownership language, missing legal description, or improper transfer can sit unnoticed in the land records for years.

Too often, title defects are not discovered until someone passes away, sells the property, refinances, or tries to transfer the home to a family member. By then, the problem can be more expensive, more stressful, and more difficult to fix.

Having the deed prepared by an attorney helps protect the transaction, reduce the risk of future title problems, and give your family cleaner records going forward.
